# Maine Practice Exam 

### Core Insurance Concepts (25 Questions)

---

### **Question 1**

Which of the following best describes the primary purpose of insurance?

A. To eliminate risk
B. To transfer risk from the insurer to the insured
C. To spread the financial impact of risk among many
D. To prevent losses from occurring

**Correct Answer:** C
**Rationale:** Insurance does not eliminate or prevent risk; it spreads financial loss across a pool.

---

### **Question 2**

Which of the following is an example of a speculative risk?

A. Theft of property
B. Death
C. Fire damage
D. Starting a new business

**Correct Answer:** D
**Rationale:** Speculative risk involves the possibility of gain or loss; insurance only covers pure risks.

---

### **Question 3**

Which element must be present for a risk to be insurable?

A. Loss must be catastrophic
B. Loss must be intentional
C. Loss must be measurable
D. Loss must affect only one individual

**Correct Answer:** C
**Rationale:** Insurers must be able to measure loss to price coverage.

---

### **Question 4**

What does the principle of indemnity ensure?

A. The insured profits from a loss
B. The insurer controls the insured’s actions
C. The insured is restored to their financial position prior to loss
D. The insurer pays all losses regardless of limits

**Correct Answer:** C

---

### **Question 5**

Which type of hazard arises from dishonesty or character defects?

A. Physical hazard

C. Morale hazard
